PlanetCred Climate Action Platform 🌍

A cross-platform climate action platform that enables users to document, verify, and showcase environmental contributions through a trusted portfolio system.

Design files not publicly available
Ongoing
Duration
Sole
Designer
Web & Mobile

Real-world

🔗 Explore the Project

Design files and detailed mockups are not publicly available, as this project is part of ongoing work with a company.

🔍

Discover

PlanetCred was designed to solve the problem of undocumented climate action. Many individuals contribute to environmental sustainability, but their efforts are not recorded or verified. Organizations also struggle to track real impact.

The platform bridges this gap by enabling users to submit evidence of their actions and receive verified recognition.

👤

Define - User Context

Primary Users

  • Climate-conscious individuals (Champions)
  • Organizations managing environmental initiatives

Goals

  • Document and verify climate actions
  • Build a credible environmental portfolio

Frustrations

  • Lack of recognition for efforts
  • No trusted way to prove impact
😡

Pain Points

  • 01Difficulty proving real-world environmental impact
  • 02Lack of structured systems for tracking contributions
  • 03Low trust in self-reported climate actions
  • 04Disconnection between individuals and organizations
♿

CLIMATE FLOW: ACTION TO VERIFIED IMPACT

Designing a seamless journey from participation to certification.

Discover Mission
→
Take Action
→
Submit Evidence
→
Get Verified
→
Earn Certificate
💡

Ideate - Key Design Decisions

  • 📌Structured submission flow to ensure credible verification
  • ★Use of a mascot to guide and engage users emotionally
  • 💬Clear mission categories for easy participation
  • 🏆Certificate system to reward and validate contributions
  • 📱Simple, mobile-first interaction patterns
  • 🔄Seamless continuity between web and mobile experiences to ensure a consistent user journey across platforms
🧪

Learnings

Designing PlanetCred highlighted the importance of trust in user-generated systems. Balancing simplicity with credibility required careful structuring of flows and feedback. It also reinforced how gamification and visual guidance can improve user engagement.

📱

Discover

Customers in Nigeria struggle to find and hire skilled workers reliably. They rely on informal word-of-mouth with unpredictable pricing, trust issues, and safety concerns about inviting strangers into their homes.

👤

Define - User Persona

Amina Okafor
Age 29 • Marketing Executive • Abuja

Busy Working Mother

Needs to quickly find and book trusted home service providers. The word-of-mouth process is unreliable, time-consuming, and often unsafe for herself and her family.

"I just want someone I can trust to get the job done without stress."
Goal
Find trusted handymen fast
Frustration
No pricing transparency
Need
Safety & reliability
Context
Time-poor working mum
😡

Pain Points

  • 01Hard to verify if a handyman is skilled or reliable
  • 02Finding and booking help takes too much effort
  • 03Fear of letting unknown workers into the home
  • 04Worry that app-based services will cost more

HandyMan HandyMen Side 🛠️

The service-provider half of HandyMan — enabling skilled workers to showcase services, manage job requests, and receive secure payment within one coherent design system.

View Mockup →FigJam
2 Wks
Duration
Sole
Designer
Web App
Platform
B2B
Focus
🔍

Discover

Skilled workers in Nigeria lack a structured way to market their services, manage bookings, and receive secure payment. They rely on informal referrals with inconsistent income and no professional presence online.

👤

Define - The Challenge

Designing this provider side simultaneously alongside the customer flow within a 2-week hackathon. Two very different user types (service seeker vs service provider) - one coherent design system that feels like a single product.

Provider Dashboard: Wireframe to Mockup

Creating a clear, robust interface for service providers to manage jobs, see earnings, and build reputation.

1. Provider Wireframes

2. High-Fidelity Mockups

💡

Ideate - Key Features

  • ✓Service showcase profile - skills, ratings, and availability
  • ✓Job request management - accept, decline, or schedule
  • ✓Secure in-app payment for completed tasks
  • ✓Customer rating system to build reputation over time
🧪

Learnings

Balancing two user groups in a marketplace taught me to think in systems, not just screens. Every customer feature had a mirror on the provider side - keeping both coherent under time pressure was the core design challenge.

MaAmaka Inclusive Shopping 🛒

A voice-controlled e-commerce app for blind Nigerians (ages 18–55) to independently purchase local food items. Designed accessibility-first, from research through to prototype.

View Prototype →Figma File
Mobile
Platform
18–55
Age Range
Sole
Designer
A11y
Focus
🔍

Discover

Blind Nigerians are forced to depend on others to purchase local food items - creating vulnerability to being cheated, removing personal choice, and stripping independence in a fundamental daily activity.

👤

Define - User Persona

Alabisi
Age 33 • Benin City • Widowed

Mother of Two • Recently Lost Sight

Depends on neighbours and family for groceries. Feels like a burden, suspects she is regularly overcharged, and wants the same shopping independence she had before losing her sight.

"Sometimes, I feel people cheat me when they help me get items."
Goal
Shop independently
Frustration
Feels cheated & helpless
Need
Price comparison + choice
Context
2 young children, widowed
😡

Pain Points

  • 01Forced dependency on others for everyday shopping
  • 02Must accept items whether she likes them or not
  • 03Vulnerable to being cheated without knowing it
  • 04No ability to compare items or prices independently
🔎

Research Method

Qualitative interviews focused on navigation expectations, cart interactions, checkout, search, and overall app experience. A simulation approach was used - asking a sighted person to simulate blindness and answer from that perspective.

Accessible Voice Flow: Wireframe to Mockup

Translating low-fidelity paper and digital structural flows into high-contrast, large-touch-target accessible mobile interfaces.

  • 👪Non-blind relatives considered as secondary users

Student • AKUM University

Undergraduate • On-The-Go

Wants to register courses without being physically on campus. Prefers completing registration immediately after payment - even before resumption - so she can focus on other areas of her life.

"I want to be able to register my courses seamlessly even while on the go."
Goal
Register remotely
Frustration
Physical forms & long walks
Need
Do it before resumption
Context
Active student, always busy
😡

Pain Points

  • 01Students strongly dislike the pen-and-paper process
  • 02Must walk distances to collect forms and get advisor signatures
  • 03Difficult to safely keep and track physical registration forms
Digital Registration Flow: Wireframe to Mockup

2. High-Fidelity Mockups
